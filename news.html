Publication

New Paper on Physics-Informed Neural Networks for VIV Published

Our latest research on using Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) for predicting vortex-induced vibrations has been accepted for publication in the Journal of Fluids and Structures. The work demonstrates a 3-4 orders of magnitude speedup compared to traditional CFD-FEM simulations while maintaining excellent accuracy. This breakthrough enables real-time VIV prediction for offshore riser design and monitoring.

Presentation at OMAE 2024 Conference

Successfully presented research findings on helical groove VIV suppression devices at the ASME OMAE 2024 conference in Singapore. The presentation highlighted our computational and experimental work showing up to 70% reduction in cross-flow vibration amplitudes. Received excellent feedback from industry practitioners and academic researchers, opening potential collaboration opportunities.

November 22, 2024
Award

Best Paper Award at Marine Structures Symposium

Honored to receive the Best Paper Award at the International Symposium on Marine Structures for our work on reduced-order modeling of fluid-structure interaction in offshore risers. The award recognizes the innovative approach combining proper orthogonal decomposition with machine learning for efficient FSI prediction. Grateful to my advisors and collaborators for their support.

October 5, 2024
Project

New Research Grant Awarded for VIV Energy Harvesting

Excited to announce that our proposal on VIV-based energy harvesting for powering ocean sensors has been funded by the Department of Science and Technology. The three-year project will investigate optimal design parameters, stability analysis, and field testing of energy harvesting devices in marine environments. This work has the potential to enable self-powered ocean monitoring systems.

September 18, 2024
Publication

Journal Article on Wake Oscillator Calibration Accepted

Our paper on improved wake oscillator model calibration for VIV prediction has been accepted in Ocean Engineering. The work presents a systematic calibration framework using high-fidelity CFD data and demonstrates improved prediction of higher harmonic responses and transition between response branches. The calibrated models are being made available as open-source code for the research community.
