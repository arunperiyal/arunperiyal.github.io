Teaching Philosophy

I believe in combining theoretical rigor with practical applications to make complex engineering concepts accessible and engaging. My teaching approach emphasizes hands-on learning through computational projects, case studies from real-world marine engineering problems, and interactive demonstrations. I strive to foster critical thinking and problem-solving skills that will serve students throughout their careers.

Tutorials and Workshops

Introduction to OpenFOAM for Marine Applications

A comprehensive tutorial series covering OpenFOAM setup, mesh generation, solver selection, and post-processing for typical marine engineering problems including ship resistance, wave-structure interaction, and VIV simulations.

  • Tutorial 1: OpenFOAM Installation and Basic Cases
  • Tutorial 2: Mesh Generation with blockMesh and snappyHexMesh
  • Tutorial 3: Multiphase Flows and Wave Modeling
  • Tutorial 4: Moving Mesh and FSI Coupling
  • Tutorial 5: Post-processing and Visualization
Access Tutorials →

Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) Workshop

Hands-on workshop introducing PINNs for solving PDEs and fluid-structure interaction problems. Includes theory, implementation in PyTorch/TensorFlow, and applications to marine engineering case studies.

  • Session 1: Neural Network Fundamentals and Automatic Differentiation
  • Session 2: Incorporating Physics Constraints into Loss Functions
  • Session 3: Solving Navier-Stokes Equations with PINNs
  • Session 4: Applications to VIV and Structural Dynamics
Access Workshop Materials →

Marine Riser Design and Analysis

Practical guide to marine riser design covering loads, structural analysis, VIV assessment, and fatigue calculations using industry-standard tools and approaches.

  • Module 1: Riser Types and Configurations
  • Module 2: Environmental Loading and Current Profiles
  • Module 3: Static and Dynamic Analysis
  • Module 4: VIV Prediction and Suppression
  • Module 5: Fatigue Life Assessment

Student Projects

I supervise undergraduate and graduate research projects in areas including:

  • Computational fluid dynamics and fluid-structure interaction
  • Machine learning applications in marine engineering
  • Vortex-induced vibration suppression and energy harvesting
  • Reduced-order modeling and surrogate modeling
  • Wave energy conversion and renewable ocean energy
  • Ship hydrodynamics and seakeeping analysis

If you're interested in working on any of these topics, please feel free to contact me.
